The Ixol Alphabet and Pronunciation

Ixol uses the Latin alphabet and is **fully phonetic** â€“ every letter is pronounced consistently, and words are read exactly as they are spelled.

Consonants

Most consonants in Ixol are pronounced similarly to their English equivalents, but with a slight Filipino language feel:

  • b, d, f, g, h, k, l, m, n, p, s, t, v, w, y: Generally pronounced like their English counterparts.
  • g: Always a hard 'g' sound, like in "go".
  • s: Always a soft 's' sound, like in "snake".
  • w: Like the 'w' in "water".
  • y: Like the 'y' in "yes".
  • j: Pronounced like the 'j' in "jam" or the 'dy' sound in "soldier".
  • r: Can be a soft tap or a slight roll of the tongue, similar to the 'r' in many Filipino languages or Spanish.
  • x: Pronounced like 'ks', as in "box".
  • Combinations - ng: When 'n' and 'g' appear together, they form the 'ng' sound as in "sing" or "finger". This is a very common sound in Filipino languages.

Vowels

The vowels in Ixol are pronounced clearly and consistently, similar to how they sound in Spanish or Filipino languages:

  • a: like the 'a' in **fa**ther
  • e: like the 'e' in b**e**d
  • i: like the 'ee' in m**ee**t
  • o: like the 'o' in g**o**
  • u: like the 'oo' in m**oo**n

Basic Grammar: Simple Structure

Ixol's grammar aims for simplicity, focusing on clear communication.

Word Order

Ixol primarily uses a **Verb-Subject-Object (VSO)** word order. This means the verb usually comes first, followed by the subject, and then the object. This is a common structure in Filipino languages.

Example: "Eat I food." (Instead of "I eat food.")

Nouns

Nouns in Ixol are straightforward. They do not have grammatical gender.

Pluralization:

To indicate a plural noun, we use the particle **ma** placed before the noun.

  • adlaw (day) → ma adlaw (days)
  • pakan (food) → ma pakan (foods)

Verbs

Verbs in Ixol start simply, focusing on the action. To indicate tense, we use small particles placed before the verb.

  • Present Tense: Use the base form of the verb.
  • Past Tense: Add **na** before the verb.
  • Future Tense: Add **ka** before the verb.

Pronouns

  • Akon (I/Me)
  • Ikay (You)
  • Siya (He/She/It)
  • Silay (They)

Adjectives

Adjectives in Ixol generally come after the noun they describe. This is a common pattern in Filipino languages.

  • pakan mabaw (good food)
  • tubig inat (hot water)

Questions

To form a simple yes/no question, place the particle **ba** at the beginning of the sentence. For "what" questions, use **unsay**.

  • Ba kanan ikay pakan? (Do you eat food?)
  • Unsay inay? (What is this?)
